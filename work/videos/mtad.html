about this project

My Time at Deepwater: a Retrospective is a social commentary over the poor treatment of my junior high by students from our sister schools. The video was created as an English project in May of 2020, a bit after the first wave of COVID-19 hit; as such, I produced it entirely at home.
The assignment stated I was allowed to criticize the school distict and its schools, if I liked. I realized this was finally an opportunity to give my friends a voice against the others, as we were seen as inferior and just stupid because our school, Deepwater, was primarily attended by Hispanic students.

creating this project

For the longest time, this project was just sitting on my to-do list. I knew I wanted to make a video for the assignment, and I had written the introductory paragraph in Notion. I created a small title animation using the school logos and dropped it into Premiere. I did some research about school statistics and browsed for pictures I could use as visual aid to the intro. I'd also asked a few friends about some significant memories of discrimination they'd experienced in our junior high years and written some testimony, but that was all.
Then around four days before it was finally due, I decided to double down on it, and crunched the script out in one night. I didn't have a professional mic, so I went into my closet to record the voiceover on my phone in my closet.
At this point, it was a weekend before the video needed to be ready to submit on Monday night. I shifted into overdrive and started to edit non-stop for two days. I worked on each chapter of the video before moving on to the next. I trimmed pops from my voiceover and timed it to the music tracks I had picked out. Then, I started to gather the visuals for what I would be saying at what time. I had already gatherd some information of the schools' student stats from public educational record sites. I looked mostly for stock footage to pair with the words.
I rendered a couple of test clips and sent them to a few of my close friends to gather feedback of what I should change. I also finalized the testimony I'd gotten and made After Effects comps for the quotes to scroll along with the voiceover.
Monday night, at around ten o'clock, I published and submitted the final video. The video's original audience was pretty much my English teacher and a few of my friends that attended Deepwater with me, but its reach went far beyond that. I began receiving praise from peers from all over the district, old teachers, and even school board members, who had somehow obtained it.
This video is one I'm quite proud of. Creating it with a passion to give Deepwater kids a voice helped its fruition greatly.

takeaways from this project

what I learned
- This was my first time working in Audition where I used the mixes in an overarching project
- This was also the first time I used the big four Adobe programs in tandem with one another, utilizing Dynamic Link to make the process quicker
- I found an efficient Notion workflow to organize my script, research, b-roll/visual list, and video checklist in one place


mistakes I made
- The voiceover mixing and my voice's energy varies througout the video
- The stroke I applied to the schools' logos ruined the form, and didn't look very nice in general
- I started a bit too late, probably. Had I given myself even a few more days, I could've polished up the video more
