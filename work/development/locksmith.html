about this project

My Time at Deepwater: a Retrospective is a social commentary over the poor treatment of my junior high by students from our sister schools. The video was created as an English project in May of 2020, a bit after the first wave of COVID-19 hit; as such, I produced it entirely at home.
The assignment stated I was allowed to criticize the school distict and its schools, if I liked. I realized this was finally an opportunity to give my friends a voice against the others, as we were seen as inferior and just stupid because our school, Deepwater, was primarily attended by Hispanic students.

creating this project

I knew I wanted to make a video for the assignment, and I had written the introductory paragraph in Notion. I created a small title animation using the school logos by making a layout in Photoshop and keyframing the layers in After Effects, and then dropped it into Premiere. I did some research about school statistics and browsed for pictures I could use as visual aid to the intro. I'd also asked a few friends about some significant memories of discrimination they'd experienced in our junior high years and written down their testimony, but that was all I'd done for a while.
Then around four days before it was due, I crunched the script out in one night. I didn't have a professional mic, so I went into my closet to record the voiceover on my phone.
At this point, it was a weekend before the video needed to be ready to submit on Monday night, so I started to edit non-stop for two days. I worked on each chapter of the video before moving on to the next. I trimmed the pops from my voice clips and timed them to the music tracks I had picked out.
I rendered a couple of test clips and sent them to a few of my close friends to gather feedback of what I should change. I also finalized the testimony I'd gotten and made After Effects comps for the quotes to scroll along with the voiceover.
At ten o'clock on Monday night, I published and submitted the final video. The video's original intended audience was my English teacher and a few of my friends that attended Deepwater with me, but its reach went far beyond that. I began receiving praise from peers from all over the district, old teachers, and even school board members, which was very humbling.
This video is one I'm quite proud of. My passion to give us Deepwater kids a voice helped it come to fruition.

takeaways from this project

what I learned
- This was my first time working in Audition where I used the mixes in an overarching project
- This was also the first time I used the big four Adobe programs in tandem with one another, utilizing Dynamic Link to make the process quicker
- I found an efficient Notion workflow to organize my script, research, b-roll/visual list, and video checklist in one place


mistakes I made
- The voiceover mixing and my voice's energy varies througout the video
- The stroke I applied to the schools' logos ruined the form, and didn't look very nice in general
- I started a bit too late, probably. Had I given myself even a few more days, I could've polished up the video more
